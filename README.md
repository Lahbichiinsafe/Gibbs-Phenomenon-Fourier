# Gibbs Phenomenon and Fourier Series

## Overview
This project studies the Gibbs phenomenon, which appears when discontinuous periodic functions are approximated by Fourier series.

It combines theoretical analysis and numerical visualization to illustrate the persistence of oscillations near discontinuities.

## Objectives
- Recall the basics of periodic functions and Fourier series
- Study convergence properties of Fourier series
- Illustrate the Gibbs phenomenon on discontinuous functions
- Compare continuous and discontinuous cases
- Support the theory with numerical simulations in Python

## Content
The project includes:
- a theoretical study of Fourier coefficients and convergence
- examples of continuous and discontinuous periodic functions
- numerical plots of partial Fourier sums
- a detailed written report in LaTeX

## Numerical Illustrations
The simulations show:
- uniform convergence in the continuous case
- persistent overshoots near jump discontinuities
- the characteristic Gibbs overshoot of about 9% of the jump height

 ## Key Observations

- Convergence for continuous functions
- Persistent overshoot near discontinuities (~9%)
- Visualization confirms theoretical results
